Return on Equity Calculation

Our ROE demonstrates efficient capital utilization:

\[ROE = \frac{\text{Net Income}}{\text{Shareholder Equity}} = \frac{625M}{3.2B} = 19.5\%\]

DuPont Analysis

\[ROE = \frac{\text{Net Income}}{\text{Revenue}} \times \frac{\text{Revenue}}{\text{Assets}} \times \frac{\text{Assets}}{\text{Equity}}\]

\[= 14.9\% \times 0.87 \times 1.5 = 19.5\%\]

Our 19.5% ROE is above industry average of 15%. The DuPont breakdown shows this is driven by strong profit margins (14.9%) and efficient asset utilization (0.87 turnover), with moderate leverage (1.5x). This is a healthy balance.

Risk-Adjusted Performance

Sharpe Ratio measures risk-adjusted returns:

\[S = \frac{R_p - R_f}{\sigma_p}\]

Where:

  • \(R_p\) = Portfolio return (19.5%)
  • \(R_f\) = Risk-free rate (4.5%)
  • \(\sigma_p\) = Standard deviation (8.2%)
\[S = \frac{0.195 - 0.045}{0.082} = 1.83\]

Interpretation: Sharpe ratio of 1.83 indicates excellent risk-adjusted returns

The Sharpe ratio of 1.83 is exceptional - anything above 1.0 is considered good, above 2.0 is very good. This shows we're generating strong returns without taking excessive risk. Compare this to our peer group average of 1.2.

Customer Segmentation Query

Database query used for customer analysis:


-- Identify high-value customer segments
SELECT 
    customer_segment,
    COUNT(*) as customer_count,
    AVG(account_balance) as avg_balance,
    SUM(quarterly_revenue) as total_revenue,
    AVG(transaction_frequency) as avg_transactions
FROM customer_analytics
WHERE quarter = 'Q4_2024'
GROUP BY customer_segment
HAVING total_revenue > 1000000
ORDER BY total_revenue DESC;

This analysis identified our top 3 segments contributing 68% of total revenue

This SQL query is crucial for our segmentation strategy. Lines 1-3: select key metrics. Lines 5-8: calculate aggregates per segment. Lines 10-12: filter and sort by revenue. Results show premium and corporate segments drive majority of revenue.
